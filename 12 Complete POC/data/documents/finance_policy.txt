FINANCE AND EXPENSE POLICY — ACME CORPORATION
Version 2.1 | Last Updated: February 2024
Access Level: Manager
Department: Finance

====================================================
SECTION 1: PURPOSE
====================================================

This policy governs how employees incur, document, and submit business expenses. All employees who incur expenses on behalf of ACME Corporation must follow these procedures.

====================================================
SECTION 2: EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT
====================================================

2.1 SUBMISSION DEADLINE
All expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred. Expenses submitted after 30 days may not be reimbursed.

2.2 REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION
All expenses require:
- Original receipt (digital copies accepted)
- Business purpose stated clearly
- Names of attendees for meals/entertainment
- Manager approval for expenses over $100

2.3 REIMBURSEMENT TIMELINE
Approved expense reports are reimbursed within 15 business days via direct deposit to the employee's paycheck.

====================================================
SECTION 3: TRAVEL POLICY
====================================================

3.1 BOOKING REQUIREMENTS
All business travel must be booked through the company's approved travel portal (travel.acme-corp.com) or via the corporate travel agency.

3.2 AIR TRAVEL
- Domestic flights: Economy class required
- International flights over 8 hours: Business class permitted with VP approval
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible to minimize costs
- Maximum flight cost: $800 domestic, $3,000 international

3.3 HOTELS
- Book approved hotels from the preferred vendor list
- Standard rate cap: $200/night domestic, $350/night international
- Premium cities (New York, San Francisco, London): $300 domestic cap

3.4 PER DIEM RATES
Daily meal allowances (receipts NOT required for amounts under the per diem):
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $25
- Dinner: $50
- Incidentals (tips, transport, etc.): $25/day

Total daily per diem: $120

3.5 CAR RENTALS
- Economy or compact vehicles only
- Always purchase the collision damage waiver (CDW)
- Mileage reimbursement for personal vehicle use: $0.67/mile (IRS rate)

====================================================
SECTION 4: PURCHASE AUTHORIZATION LIMITS
====================================================

4.1 EMPLOYEE SPENDING LIMITS
Individual employees may approve the following without additional sign-off:
- Under $100:     No approval required
- $100 - $500:    Direct manager approval
- $500 - $2,000:  Department head approval
- $2,000 - $10,000: Director-level approval
- Over $10,000:   VP + Finance team approval

4.2 PROHIBITED EXPENSES
The following will NEVER be reimbursed:
- Alcohol (unless part of approved client entertainment with VP approval)
- Personal expenses (gym, personal meals not during travel, etc.)
- Traffic fines, parking tickets
- First-class domestic air travel
- Personal vehicle insurance
- Spouse/partner travel (unless specifically approved)

====================================================
SECTION 5: CORPORATE CREDIT CARDS
====================================================

5.1 ELIGIBILITY
Corporate credit cards are issued to:
- All managers and above (automatic)
- Employees who travel more than 4 times per year
- Employees with documented need (approved by HR + Finance)

5.2 CARD USAGE
Corporate cards are for business expenses ONLY. Personal use is grounds for disciplinary action and immediate card revocation.

5.3 MONTHLY RECONCILIATION
All corporate card holders must reconcile their card statement by the 5th business day of each month.

====================================================
SECTION 6: BUDGET MANAGEMENT
====================================================

6.1 BUDGET CYCLE
The annual budget cycle runs from October to December for the following fiscal year. Department heads submit budget requests using the standard template.

6.2 BUDGET APPROVAL HIERARCHY
- Department budgets: approved by VP of Finance
- Project budgets over $50K: requires C-level approval
- Unbudgeted items: require one level above standard purchase authority

6.3 QUARTERLY REVIEWS
Finance holds quarterly budget reviews with all department heads to track spending versus plan and address variances.

====================================================
SECTION 7: ANNUAL BONUS STRUCTURE
====================================================
(CONFIDENTIAL — Manager and above access only)

7.1 BONUS ELIGIBILITY
All full-time employees who have been employed for at least 6 months during the performance year are eligible for an annual performance bonus.

7.2 BONUS CALCULATION
Base bonus percentage by role level:
- Individual Contributor: 5% - 10% of annual salary
- Senior Individual Contributor: 8% - 15% of annual salary
- Manager: 10% - 20% of annual salary
- Senior Manager / Director: 15% - 25% of annual salary
- VP and above: 20% - 40% of annual salary

7.3 PERFORMANCE MULTIPLIER
The bonus percentage is modified by the annual performance rating:
- Rating 1 (Needs Improvement): 0x multiplier (no bonus)
- Rating 2 (Developing): 0.5x multiplier
- Rating 3 (Meets Expectations): 1.0x multiplier
- Rating 4 (Exceeds Expectations): 1.25x multiplier
- Rating 5 (Exceptional): 1.5x multiplier

Example: Manager with $80,000 salary, 15% base bonus, Rating 4:
$80,000 × 15% × 1.25 = $15,000 bonus

7.4 COMPANY PERFORMANCE MODIFIER
In addition to individual performance, the company applies a multiplier based on overall company performance:
- Below 80% of targets: 0.75x
- 80-99% of targets: 1.0x
- 100%+ of targets: 1.1x - 1.25x
