IT SECURITY POLICY — ACME CORPORATION
Version 3.0 | Last Updated: March 2024
Access Level: Public
Department: IT

====================================================
SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE
====================================================

This policy establishes security requirements for all employees, contractors, and vendors who access ACME Corporation's information systems, networks, and data.

Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

====================================================
SECTION 2: PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS
====================================================

2.1 PASSWORD STANDARDS
All passwords must:
- Be at least 12 characters long
- Include at least one uppercase letter
- Include at least one lowercase letter
- Include at least one number
- Include at least one special character (!@#$%^&*)
- Not contain your name, username, or common words

2.2 PASSWORD ROTATION
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Cannot reuse the last 10 passwords
- Passwords may not be written down or shared

2.3 MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (MFA)
MFA is required for:
- All email and company systems
- VPN access
- Any system containing customer data
- All admin-level accounts

Approved MFA methods: authenticator app (preferred), SMS (fallback)

====================================================
SECTION 3: ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
====================================================

3.1 COMPANY EQUIPMENT
Company computers, phones, and other equipment are provided for business use. Limited personal use is permitted but:
- Must not interfere with work duties
- Must comply with this policy
- Must not install unauthorized software

3.2 PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
The following are strictly prohibited on company systems:
- Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content
- Using company systems to run personal businesses
- Bypassing security controls or firewalls
- Mining cryptocurrency
- Accessing peer-to-peer file sharing networks
- Installing unauthorized software or applications

3.3 MONITORING
Company systems are monitored for security purposes. Employees have no expectation of privacy on company systems.

====================================================
SECTION 4: DATA CLASSIFICATION
====================================================

4.1 CLASSIFICATION LEVELS

PUBLIC
- Information approved for public distribution
- Examples: marketing materials, press releases, public website content
- Can be shared externally without restriction

INTERNAL
- Day-to-day business information
- Examples: internal memos, meeting notes, project plans
- May be shared internally; external sharing requires manager approval

CONFIDENTIAL
- Sensitive business information
- Examples: financial reports, customer data, employee records
- Must not be shared externally without VP approval
- Must be encrypted when stored or transmitted

RESTRICTED
- Most sensitive information
- Examples: trade secrets, acquisition plans, C-level compensation
- Access strictly controlled on need-to-know basis
- Must be encrypted and access logged

4.2 DATA HANDLING REQUIREMENTS
- Confidential and Restricted data must be encrypted at rest and in transit
- Never store sensitive data on personal devices
- Use company-approved cloud storage only (SharePoint, Teams)
- Do not email Restricted data without encryption

====================================================
SECTION 5: REMOTE ACCESS AND VPN
====================================================

5.1 VPN REQUIREMENT
Employees must use the company VPN when:
- Accessing any internal systems remotely
- Working from public Wi-Fi networks
- Accessing any Confidential or Restricted data remotely

5.2 HOME NETWORK SECURITY
When working from home:
- Use a secured, password-protected Wi-Fi network
- Keep your home router firmware up to date
- Do not allow others to use company equipment

====================================================
SECTION 6: INCIDENT REPORTING
====================================================

6.1 WHAT TO REPORT
Report immediately to IT Security (security@acme-corp.com):
- Suspected phishing emails
- Malware or ransomware
- Unauthorized access to systems
- Lost or stolen company devices
- Any security breach or suspicious activity

6.2 RESPONSE TIME
IT Security will respond to:
- Critical incidents: within 1 hour
- High severity: within 4 hours
- Medium/Low severity: within 24 hours

6.3 NON-RETALIATION
Employees who report security incidents in good faith will not face retaliation.

====================================================
SECTION 7: SOFTWARE AND DEVICE MANAGEMENT
====================================================

7.1 APPROVED SOFTWARE
All software installed on company devices must be:
- Approved by IT (see the approved software list at it.acme-corp.com)
- Licensed appropriately
- Kept up to date with security patches

7.2 PERSONAL DEVICES (BYOD)
Personal devices may be used for email with IT approval. Requirements:
- Device must have MDM (Mobile Device Management) enrolled
- Device must have current OS version
- Device must have screen lock enabled
- Company data must be encrypted on the device

7.3 DEVICE ENCRYPTION
All company laptops and mobile devices must have:
- Full-disk encryption enabled (FileVault on Mac, BitLocker on Windows)
- Remote wipe capability configured

====================================================
SECTION 8: PHYSICAL SECURITY
====================================================

8.1 OFFICE ACCESS
- ID badges must be worn visibly at all times in office
- Do not allow tailgating (following someone through a secure door)
- Report lost badges immediately to Facilities

8.2 CLEAN DESK POLICY
- Lock computer screens when leaving your desk
- Do not leave sensitive documents visible at your desk
- Shred sensitive printed documents before disposal

====================================================
END OF IT SECURITY POLICY
