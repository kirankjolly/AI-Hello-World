HR HANDBOOK — ACME CORPORATION
Version 4.2 | Last Updated: January 2024
Access Level: Public

====================================================
SECTION 1: WELCOME AND INTRODUCTION
====================================================

Welcome to ACME Corporation. This handbook describes your rights, benefits, and responsibilities as an employee. Please read it carefully and keep a copy for your reference.

This handbook applies to all full-time and part-time employees. Contractors and consultants are subject to their contract terms.

====================================================
SECTION 2: EMPLOYMENT POLICIES
====================================================

2.1 AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT
Employment at ACME Corporation is at-will. This means either the employee or the company may end the employment relationship at any time, for any lawful reason, with or without notice.

2.2 EQUAL OPPORTUNITY
ACME Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

2.3 BACKGROUND CHECKS
All employment offers are conditional upon successful completion of a background check and, where applicable, a drug screening.

====================================================
SECTION 3: COMPENSATION AND PAYROLL
====================================================

3.1 PAYROLL SCHEDULE
Employees are paid bi-weekly (every two weeks) on Fridays. There are 26 pay periods in a year.

3.2 DIRECT DEPOSIT
Direct deposit is required for all employees. Contact HR to set up or change your banking information.

3.3 OVERTIME
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x their regular rate for hours worked beyond 40 per week. Overtime must be pre-approved by a manager.

3.4 BONUS PROGRAM
The company offers performance-based bonuses. Annual bonuses are typically paid in Q1 of the following year. The standard bonus range is 5% to 20% of annual salary based on individual and company performance.

====================================================
SECTION 4: VACATION AND TIME OFF
====================================================

4.1 VACATION DAYS
Full-time employees receive the following paid vacation days per year:
- Years 0-4:   15 vacation days per year
- Years 5-9:   20 vacation days per year
- Years 10+:   25 vacation days per year

Part-time employees receive pro-rated vacation days based on their scheduled hours.

4.2 VACATION ACCRUAL
Vacation days accrue monthly:
- 15-day policy: 1.25 days per month
- 20-day policy: 1.67 days per month
- 25-day policy: 2.08 days per month

New employees begin accruing vacation on their first day.

4.3 VACATION CARRYOVER
A maximum of 5 unused vacation days may be carried over to the next calendar year. Unused days above this limit are forfeited on December 31st.

4.4 VACATION APPROVAL
Vacation requests must be submitted in writing (via the HR portal) at least 2 weeks in advance. For vacation periods longer than 5 consecutive days, 4 weeks' notice is recommended. Final approval rests with the employee's direct manager.

====================================================
SECTION 5: SICK LEAVE
====================================================

5.1 SICK LEAVE ALLOCATION
All employees (full-time and part-time) receive 10 sick days per calendar year. Sick days are available immediately from the first day of employment and do not accrue monthly.

5.2 USE OF SICK LEAVE
Sick leave may be used for:
- Employee's own illness or injury
- Medical appointments
- Caring for an immediate family member who is ill
- Mental health days (up to 3 per year)

5.3 DOCTOR'S NOTE
A doctor's note is required for sick absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

5.4 CARRYOVER
Sick days do NOT carry over to the next year. They reset on January 1st.

====================================================
SECTION 6: PARENTAL LEAVE
====================================================

6.1 PRIMARY CAREGIVER LEAVE
The primary caregiver (birth parent, adoptive parent, or primary caregiver) receives 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave.

6.2 SECONDARY CAREGIVER LEAVE
The secondary caregiver receives 6 weeks of fully paid parental leave.

6.3 ELIGIBILITY
Parental leave applies to:
- Birth of a biological child
- Legal adoption
- Placement for foster care (with intent to adopt)

Employees are eligible after 6 months of employment.

6.4 NOTICE
Please notify HR at least 30 days in advance when possible.

====================================================
SECTION 7: REMOTE WORK POLICY
====================================================

7.1 HYBRID WORK SCHEDULE
ACME Corporation operates a hybrid work model. Employees may work remotely up to 3 days per week.

7.2 REQUIRED IN-OFFICE DAYS
All employees must be in the office on:
- Tuesday
- Thursday
These are designated "core collaboration days."

7.3 HOME OFFICE REIMBURSEMENT
The company provides:
- Up to $500 per year for home office equipment (chair, desk, monitor, etc.)
- $50/month internet stipend for remote work expenses

Submit receipts via the expense management portal within 30 days of purchase.

====================================================
SECTION 8: CODE OF CONDUCT
====================================================

8.1 PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
All employees are expected to behave professionally, treat colleagues with respect, and contribute positively to the workplace culture.

8.2 ANTI-HARASSMENT POLICY
Harassment of any kind — sexual, racial, gender-based, or otherwise — is strictly prohibited. Violations are grounds for immediate termination.

8.3 CONFLICT OF INTEREST
Employees must disclose any outside employment or business interests that may conflict with their duties at ACME Corporation. Written disclosure is required.

====================================================
SECTION 9: PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
====================================================

9.1 ANNUAL REVIEW
Performance reviews are conducted annually in December. Reviews assess performance against goals set at the beginning of the year.

9.2 MID-YEAR CHECK-IN
A mid-year check-in is held in June to discuss progress, adjust goals, and provide early feedback.

9.3 RATING SCALE
Performance is rated on a 5-point scale:
  1 - Needs Improvement
  2 - Developing
  3 - Meets Expectations
  4 - Exceeds Expectations
  5 - Exceptional

9.4 PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)
Employees rated 1 (Needs Improvement) two consecutive years may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan. Details will be discussed with HR.

====================================================
SECTION 10: BENEFITS OVERVIEW
====================================================

10.1 HEALTH INSURANCE
The company covers 80% of health insurance premiums for employees and 60% for dependents. Plans offered: Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum.

10.2 DENTAL AND VISION
Dental: Company covers 70% of premiums.
Vision: Company covers 60% of premiums.
Annual vision allowance: $200 for glasses or contacts.

10.3 401(K) RETIREMENT PLAN
The company matches 100% of employee contributions up to 4% of salary. Vesting schedule: 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years.

10.4 LIFE INSURANCE
Basic life insurance equal to 2x annual salary is provided at no cost. Supplemental coverage available at employee's expense.

====================================================
SECTION 11: PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
====================================================

11.1 EDUCATION REIMBURSEMENT
Employees may receive up to $3,000 per year for approved job-related education (courses, certifications, conferences). Requires manager approval before enrollment.

11.2 LEARNING BUDGET
In addition to formal education, each employee has a $500/year learning budget for books, online courses, and workshops.
